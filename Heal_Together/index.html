Raising Awareness About Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers affecting women worldwide, but early detection can save lives. Regular screenings, self-examinations, and awareness of symptoms are key to early diagnosis and better treatment outcomes.

Breast cancer happens when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the breast. It mostly affects women, with risk factors including genetics, lifestyle, and hormones.

  • Be aware of lumps or thickening in the breast or underarm.
  • Watch for changes in the size, shape, or texture of the breast skin.
  • Take note of any unexplained pain or discharge.
  • Perform regular self-exams to check for changes.
  • Schedule routine mammograms for early detection.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle to reduce risk factors.

We Offer Different Services To Improve Your Health

We’re here for you with advanced testing solutions, helping you stay informed and take control of your health.

Positron Emission Tomography (PET) Scan

A nuclear imaging test that detects cancerous cells by using a radioactive tracer to highlight metabolic activity in tissues.

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Uses powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of organs and tissues, helping in cancer detection, staging, and treatment monitoring.

Flow Cytometry

A laboratory technique that analyzes cancer cells (especially blood cancers like leukemia) by passing them through a laser beam and detecting their physical and chemical properties.

Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)

A genetic test that sequences DNA or RNA of tumors to identify mutations, helping in targeted therapy selection.

Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)

A molecular test that detects genetic abnormalities in cancer cells by using fluorescent probes, often used for breast cancer (HER2) and leukemia.

Liquid Biopsy (Circulating Tumor DNA Test)

A blood test that detects tumor-derived DNA fragments, providing real-time cancer monitoring without the need for invasive biopsies.
